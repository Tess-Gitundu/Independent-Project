About Me

My Background

I am a 23-year old beautiful, confident, fun, and amazing lady who lives in Kinoo, Kikuyu. I was born and raised in Kinoo, Kikuyu. I am the last born daughter of my late father, Peter Gitundu Muna and my loving and beautiful mother, Mary Mukami Gachonde. I have two siblings, Rosemary Wanjiru Gitundu and Francis Muna Gitundu. I am also blessed with two have two beautiful nieces, Charlice Mukami and MaryAngel Mukami.

Education

I attended Mama Ngina Kenyatta Primary School in Kinoo from an early aged where I attained my Kenya Certificate of Primary Education in the year 2012. I later joined Kambui Girls High School the following year. I studied there until I attained my Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education. I am currently a student at Moringa School pursuing Software Development.

Job Experience

After graduating from high school, I worked for BlueSky Laundry and Drycleaning as a Customer Care Executive Agent for two years. I then joined the freelancers force and became a transcriptionist for about three years. In mid 2020, I decided to start a mandazi business to help me work on my confidence. I sold mandazis for a couple of months then decided to shift gears a little bit. I later started an onion-selling business which lasted a couple of months. I then started a small business, Undies By Tess which I was slowly building until I joined Moringa School.

Why Moringa School

Since I was young, I would appreciate how technology has changed lives in various ways. I loved how different fields of the economy had been affected by technology. I also appreciate the fact that it was technology that brought food to the table when I was young since my dad used to write articles for websites back in the day. Let's just say, technology has changed my life as well. I would always think of ways to make lives better through apps. I never really knew what to do with this passion, so I never really explored it until I learnt about Moringa's Women in Tech Scholarship Program. To say that was exactly what I needed at that particular point in time is an injustice. So I decided to apply and try my luck. I honestly didn't expect to get the scholarship. I mean, imagine competing with all these beautiful women out here! I couldn't believe it when I got the congratulatory email. It was a miracle! I decided to pursue Software Development to make sure I achieve this dream I'd been having for years. I'll make sure I become the best Software Engineer there is. I look forward to changing the world with technology as my weapon of change. So, look out!
